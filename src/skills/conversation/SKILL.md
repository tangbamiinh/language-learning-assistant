# Conversation Practice

A role-play skill for practicing Mandarin Chinese through real-world conversation scenarios. Designed to help Vietnamese learners build speaking confidence with structured, level-appropriate practice.

## When to Use
- When the student wants to practice speaking
- When the student says they want to do a role-play or scenario
- When the student wants to practice for a specific situation (travel, business, etc.)
- After teaching vocabulary or grammar, for practical application
- When the student asks to "practice a conversation"

## Available Scenarios

The scenarios are stored in `scenarios.json`. Categories include:

- **Greeting & Introduction** (HSK 1): 打招呼, 自我介绍
- **Restaurant** (HSK 2): 点餐, 付账
- **Shopping** (HSK 2): 买东西, 讨价还价
- **Asking Directions** (HSK 2): 问路
- **Hotel** (HSK 3): 入住, 退房
- **Hospital** (HSK 3): 看病
- **Subway/Metro** (HSK 3): 坐地铁
- **Bank** (HSK 4): 银行业务
- **Making Appointments** (HSK 3): 打电话约时间
- **Job Interview** (HSK 5): 面试

## Procedure

1. **Scenario Selection**: Present available scenarios matching the student's HSK level
2. **Setup**: Explain the scenario, assign roles, review key vocabulary
3. **Role-Play**: Engage in the conversation as the assigned character
4. **Guidance**: If the student is stuck, provide hints (Chinese → pinyin → Vietnamese)
5. **Feedback**: After the scenario, review performance:
   - What went well
   - Grammar corrections
   - Better expressions to use
   - New vocabulary learned
6. **Replay**: Offer to replay the scenario with corrections

## Role-Play Format

```
🎭 [Scenario Name] | 📍 [Setting] | 📊 HSK [Level]
👤 You: [Student's role]
🤖 Me: [Your role]

Key vocabulary:
- [word 1]: [pinyin] - [meaning]
- [word 2]: [pinyin] - [meaning]

Ready? Let's start! [Opening line in character]
```

## Teaching During Conversation
- Stay in character during the role-play
- Use appropriate language level for the student
- If the student can't find a word, hint with:
  1. First: Vietnamese equivalent or Hán Việt cognate
  2. Second: Pinyin pronunciation
  3. Third: Full Chinese phrase
- Keep the conversation flowing naturally
- Don't correct every mistake mid-conversation - note them for feedback

## Post-Scenario Feedback Format

```
📊 [Scenario Name] - Performance Review

✅ Great:
- [What the student did well]

🔧 To Improve:
- [Mistake] → [Correction with explanation]
- [Alternative expression]

📝 New Vocabulary:
- [word]: [pinyin] - [meaning]

💡 Tips for next time:
- [Specific advice]
```

## Conversation Strategies
- **Natural flow**: Don't make it feel like a test
- **Adjust difficulty**: If the student struggles, simplify. If easy, add complexity.
- **Cultural notes**: Mention cultural aspects of the conversation
- **Practical phrases**: Highlight useful phrases they can use in real life
- **Confidence building**: Celebrate when the student communicates successfully

## Bilingual Support
- During role-play: Primarily Chinese, with Vietnamese hints when needed
- During feedback: Vietnamese explanations for corrections
- Always offer to switch between languages as needed

## Pitfalls
- Don't stay in character during feedback - switch to tutor mode
- Don't make scenarios too long (5-10 exchanges is enough)
- Don't use vocabulary far above the student's level
- Avoid overly scripted conversations - allow natural variation

## Verification

After the scenario, assess:
1. Could the student communicate the main ideas?
2. Were there recurring grammar mistakes to address?
3. Did they use any of the target vocabulary?
4. Are they ready for the next level of difficulty?
